Contractor tracking platform deep dive

Contractor tracking & vetting platform

No centralised, trustworthy database of contractor performance exists in East Africa. Clients hire blindly. Bad contractors repeat failures across multiple clients with zero accountability. This platform fixes that by creating a verified track record for every registered contractor.

Core pain
A client hires a contractor based on word-of-mouth. Contractor abandons site, disappears with materials, or delivers poor work. The next client has no way to know this happened.
Who pays
Contractors pay to list & verify. Clients (individuals, developers, NGOs) pay to access detailed records. Banks & insurers pay for due diligence API access.
Regulatory angle
NCA (National Construction Authority) already registers contractors but has no performance layer. You plug directly into that gap — or eventually partner with them.
Network effect
More contractors listed → more useful to clients → more clients checking → contractors want to be verified. Classic marketplace flywheel.
What the platform tracks
1
Identity & registration verification
NCA registration number, business PIN, directors' IDs, physical address. Cross-checked against NCA public register via scraping or API.
2
Project history
Clients submit project reports — scope, contract value, start/end dates, payment disputes, and a structured rating. Contractor can respond to each report.
3
Performance scoring
Composite score across: on-time delivery, quality of workmanship, payment to sub-contractors, responsiveness, and dispute history. Weighted by project value.
4
Financial health signals
CRB status check, tax compliance certificate status (iTax), and payment history with suppliers. Optional bank statement analysis for prequalification.
5
Dispute & blacklist registry
Structured dispute logs with both-party statements. A contractor with 3+ unresolved disputes gets flagged. Public blacklist for confirmed fraud cases.
6
Document vault
Contractors upload and timestamp: insurance certificates, NCA licence, certified personnel lists, equipment inventory. Clients see what's current vs. expired.

Growth strategy
1
Seed supply
Start by listing all NCA-registered contractors (public data). Give them free basic profiles. They'll find you via Google when clients search their name.
2
Win first institutional client
Target one large developer (e.g. Unity Homes, Centum Real Estate) or an NGO with 10+ concurrent contracts. Their project submissions build your review database fast.
3
Partner with NCA or AQSK
Position as the performance layer on top of their registration system. Makes the platform semi-official and drives contractor sign-ups.
Tech stack
Next.js frontend Django / FastAPI backend PostgreSQL + full-text search M-Pesa API (payments) Africa's Talking (SMS alerts) Cloudflare R2 (document storage)
MVP scope (3 months)
1
Contractor profiles
Registration, NCA number entry, document upload, basic info. No automated verification yet — manual review queue for team.
2
Project review submission
Clients fill a structured form: project type, value, dates, 5 rating dimensions, free text. Both parties get an email to acknowledge/dispute.
3
Search & score
Public search by name or NCA number returns score + review count. Full history locked behind paywall. M-Pesa pay-per-search.
4
Admin moderation panel
Internal tool to review flagged reviews, verify documents, resolve disputes, and manage blacklist entries.
Key challenges
Fake reviews — clients and contractors both have incentives to manipulate Legal risk — a contractor listed with false negative reviews could sue Cold start — neither side uses it until the other side is already there Contractors in Kenya often operate informally — may resist being rated publicly
How to mitigate
Require contract or LPO evidence before a review is published Contractor right-of-reply on every review before it goes live Seed demand side first — get 3 institutional clients before opening to public Frame as a benefit, not surveillance — verified contractors win more tenders
