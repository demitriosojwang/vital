Three ways to integrate
1. Standalone tool for private clients — developers, NGOs, and individuals use the platform to vet contractors before inviting them to tender. No system integration needed. This is your MVP market.

2. Embedded prequalification for institutional clients — large developers or county governments use your platform as their official prequalification register. Contractors must have a verified profile to be invited to tender. Medium complexity — requires a formal MOU.

3. Tender management module — the platform extends into full tender management: publishing notices, collecting bids, running weighted scoring, and issuing award letters. This is a larger product build but creates deep lock-in and very high switching costs.
What prequalification automation looks like
Automatic eligibility check
Screens contractors before they can even submit a bid
Extends platform▾

When a procuring entity publishes a tender on the platform (or imports one), they set eligibility thresholds. The system automatically checks every bidder against these criteria and issues a pass/fail with a reason.

Checks run automatically: NCA category ≥ required category · Active NCA licence (not expired) · iTax compliance certificate valid · CRB clear (or within acceptable threshold) · No active blacklist flag · Minimum composite score (client-configurable, e.g. ≥ 55) · At least N completed projects of similar type and value · No project abandonment in past 3 years

Contractors who fail see exactly which criteria they missed — and what they need to fix to qualify for the next tender. This gives the platform a strong reason for contractors to keep their profiles current.

Standing prequalification register
Clients build a vetted shortlist once, use it repeatedly
New feature▾

Large clients (county governments, large developers, NGOs with multi-year programmes) can maintain a standing prequalified register — a list of contractors who have passed all eligibility checks and are pre-approved to bid on projects up to a defined value threshold.

The register refreshes automatically: if a contractor's NCA licence expires or their score drops below the threshold, they are removed from the register and notified. This removes the costly annual prequalification exercise most public entities run.

Revenue: Institutional clients pay an annual fee (KES 80,000–300,000) for a managed standing register. This is your highest-value institutional product.

Prequalification report PDF
Audit-ready document for procurement files
Extends platform▾

For each tender, the platform generates a prequalification report: a stamped, dated PDF listing all invited contractors, their eligibility status, scores, and pass/fail reasoning. This goes into the procurement file as evidence of due diligence.

For public sector clients, this is critical — the Public Procurement and Asset Disposal Act 2015 requires documented justification for contractor selection. The platform provides this automatically. This single feature is enough to justify the platform to a county procurement officer.

Abnormally low bid detection
Flags bids that are statistically unlikely to be deliverable
New feature▾

A contractor bidding 35% below the next lowest bid is a serious risk signal — either they have made an error, they plan to claim variations later, or they intend to use substandard materials. The platform flags bids that fall more than a configurable threshold (default: 20%) below the average of the other bids.

Flagged bids are not automatically disqualified. The procuring entity receives an alert and can request a bid price justification before proceeding. This is aligned with PPADA 2015 provisions on abnormally low tenders.

Conflict of interest detection
Cross-references directors and beneficial owners
New feature▾

When multiple contractors bid on the same tender, the platform cross-references their registered directors (from the company profiles). If two bidding companies share a director, the platform flags a potential conflict — they may be affiliated entities creating a false impression of competition.

This is especially valuable for county government tenders where bid-rigging through related companies is common. The flag goes to the tender committee, not the public.

Turning contract execution into platform data
Milestone tracking and payment certification
Client logs progress, contractor confirms — creates audit trail
New feature▾

After contract award, the client sets up milestones (e.g. substructure complete, slab poured, roofing done, practical completion). For each milestone, the client submits a progress log with a date and optional photo. The contractor confirms or disputes each log entry.

This data feeds directly into the on-time delivery and workmanship scores at completion. It also creates an auditable paper trail that protects both parties in the event of a dispute — you know exactly when each milestone was certified and by whom.

Side benefit: Payment certificates linked to milestones give the client clear justification for each payment — reducing disputes over whether a milestone was genuinely achieved before payment was released.

Early warning flags during execution
Detects distress signals before a project fails
New feature▾

The platform can surface warning signals that a contractor is in distress mid-project: a milestone is 4+ weeks overdue with no update · the contractor's CRB status changes to adverse during execution · the contractor's NCA licence lapses · a dispute is raised on a different project they are running simultaneously.

These flags are sent to the client automatically. Early warning gives the client time to intervene — issue a formal notice, bring in a supervisor, or invoke the performance bond — before the project fully collapses.

Completion review — closing the loop
Turns every project into platform data for the next tender
Extends platform▾

At practical completion, the platform prompts the client to submit a structured completion review — prefilled with the milestone data already collected. Because the data has been logged throughout the project, the review takes minutes rather than requiring the client to recall events from months ago.

This is how you build a high-quality, granular review database without asking clients to do a lot of work. The effort is distributed across the project lifecycle — not dumped onto the client at the end when they have moved on to the next thing.

The compounding effect
Every tender run on the platform generates reviews at completion → reviews improve the score database → better scores make prequalification more credible → more clients trust the platform for tendering → more tenders run → more reviews collected. The tendering module and the review database are a self-reinforcing loop. This is why extending into tendering is not just a revenue decision — it is a data strategy.
